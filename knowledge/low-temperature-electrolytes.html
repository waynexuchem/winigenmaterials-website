Low-Temperature Electrolytes

Low-Temperature Electrolyte Design for Battery Research and Extreme Environments

Low-temperature electrolyte design considerations for solvent viscosity, salt selection, lithium plating risk, ionic conductivity, and aerospace or cold-climate batteries.

Low-temperature battery operation is limited by slower ion transport, higher electrolyte viscosity, slower charge-transfer kinetics, and increased lithium plating risk. Electrolyte design for cold environments must balance conductivity, interphase stability, safety, and high-voltage compatibility.

Solvent and Salt Selection

Low-temperature electrolytes often use solvent blends that maintain lower viscosity and better ion transport at sub-zero conditions. Salt choice also matters because dissociation, solvation structure, and interphase chemistry change with temperature.

Additives and Interphase Control

Even when bulk conductivity is acceptable, poor SEI or CEI behavior can limit low-temperature performance. Additive selection can help reduce impedance growth and support more stable cycling under cold discharge or cold charge conditions.

Testing Considerations

Cold-temperature screening should separate discharge capability, charge acceptance, fast-charge behavior, and recovery at room temperature. A formulation that performs well at -20 C may not automatically support more severe -40 C or -70 C requirements.

Practical Screening Notes

  • Tune solvent viscosity, salt dissociation, and additive chemistry together.
  • Track lithium plating risk during cold charge and fast-charge protocols.
  • Compare low-temperature discharge, charge acceptance, and room-temperature recovery separately.

FAQ

Common Questions

What limits batteries at low temperature?

High viscosity, low ionic conductivity, slow interfacial kinetics, and lithium plating risk can all limit cold-temperature performance.

Can additives improve low-temperature behavior?

Yes, additives can support interphase stability, but they need to be paired with suitable solvents and salts.

Is low-temperature discharge the same as cold fast charging?

No. Cold discharge and cold charge stress different failure modes and should be tested separately.
